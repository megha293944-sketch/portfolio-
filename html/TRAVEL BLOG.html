Jatayu Earth’s Center,
world's largest bird sculpture

Jatayu Earth’s Center, located in Chadayamangalam in the Kollam district of Kerala, is a remarkable, world-record-holding destination that blends mythology, art, technology, and eco-tourism.

It is a premier attraction featuring the world's largest bird sculpture, situated atop a massive rocky hill, and dedicated to the theme of women’s safety and honor

The primary location of the statue, featuring a 360-degree panoramic view of the area, a Rama temple, and a water body believed to be formed by the impact of Jatayu's beak.



PERSONAL EXPERIENCE

The cable car ride is undoubtedly a highlight, offering a breathtaking bird's-eye view of the surrounding hills, rocky terrain, and lush greenery of Chadayamangalam

The top of the rock is serene, with a cool breeze and panoramic views that make for excellent photography, especially during sunrise or sunset. The Ram temple adds to the peaceful, spiritual vibe.

It is highly recommended to visit early in the morning or late in the afternoon to avoid the intense sun, as the main rock area is mostly exposed. Comfortable footwear is a must, and it's best to book tickets online to avoid long queues


Ashtamudi Backwaters
eight-cornered or eight-armed

Ashtamudi Backwaters, located in Kollam, Kerala, is a serene 16-km long, eight-armed lake, forming the gateway to the state’s tranquil backwaters. Surrounded by lush coconut groves and mangroves, it offers a quieter alternative to Alleppey, famous for houseboats, canoe rides, and fishing spots like the Neendakara estuary.

The name 'Ashtamudi' translates to "eight-cornered" or "eight-armed," referring to the lake's unique shape with multiple channels, making it the second-largest lake in Kerala. It feeds into the Arabian Sea through the Neendakara estuary.

Traditional kettuvallams provide luxurious,, or budget-friendly overnight stays, allowing for a slow-paced journey through, in some cases, the long, scenic route from Kollam to Alleppey.



PERSONAL EXPERIENCE

The experience is defined by profound tranquility, with, in many cases, a calm atmosphere that is ideal for relaxation, away from the bustle of typical tourist spots.

Taking a, often, 8-hour, in some cases, journey on a houseboat or a, sometimes, quiet canoe ride at sunset offers, in many cases, unforgettable views of the palm-fringed banks.

Memorable Moments: Sampling fresh, in some cases, local fish curry on a, often, houseboat and, in some cases, birdwatching (including eagles) across the expansive, in some cases, water, makes the trip, in many cases, memorable


ALLEPPEY BACK WATER
Venice of the East

Alleppey (Alappuzha) Backwaters, known as the "Venice of the East," is a serene, vast network of interconnected canals, rivers, and lagoons bordered by lush coconut groves and paddy fields. It is a quintessential Kerala experience featuring traditional houseboat cruises, vibrant village life, and fresh seafood. Visitors can enjoy navigating narrow canals, watching sunsets over Vembanad Lake, and exploring local culture.

Traditionally crafted kettuvallams (rice boats) converted into modern houseboats offer overnight or day stays. These include bedrooms, decks, and onboard chefs preparing Kerala cuisine.

The banks feature small, quaint villages where life revolves around the water. Tourists often witness local fishing, coir making, and daily routines like children going to school by boat.



PERSONAL EXPERIENCE

The experience is profoundly relaxing, allowing for a break from the hustle of city life. Watching the sunset from the deck of a houseboat is arguably the most memorable, picturesque moment.

Meals served on board, particularly the local Karimeen Pollichathu (pearl spot fish) and traditional rice meals on banana leaves, are exceptional.

A trip in a smaller canoe or Shikara allows navigating narrower canals, offering a closer look at the daily, simple lives of local residents.
